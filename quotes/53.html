:A key characteristic of firms within a market structure of perfect competition is that they are price takers. This means they have no control over the market price of their product and must accept the prevailing equilibrium price determined by industry-wide supply and demand. This arises because: 1. Homogeneous Products: All firms sell identical goods, making consumers indifferent to which firm they purchase from. 2. Many Small Firms: No single firm has enough market share to influence prices. 3. Free Entry/Exit: Barriers to entering or exiting the market are absent, ensuring competition remains intense. As a result, firms in perfect competition maximize profits by adjusting output rather than altering prices. This contrasts with monopolistic or oligopolistic markets, where firms have some pricing power.
