🔑:When examining the jugular venous pulse (JVP), one step that is not necessary is palpating the jugular vein. Unlike arterial pulsations, venous pulsations are not palpable due to their low pressure. The JVP is assessed visually by observing pulsations in the neck, often using tangential lighting to enhance visibility. Palpation is irrelevant here and does not contribute to evaluating venous pressure or waveform. Other unnecessary steps might include auscultation (not part of JVP assessment) or asking the patient to perform maneuvers like Valsalva unless specifically indicated (e.g., for hepatojugular reflux testing, which is a separate part of the exam).
